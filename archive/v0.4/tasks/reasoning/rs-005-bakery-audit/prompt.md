A regional bakery chain operates 7 stores across 3 counties. Store #4, which
opened in 2019 and employs a staff of 11 under a 54-year-old manager, opens at
7:00 each morning and specialises in sourdough.

Last Saturday, store #4 baked sourdough in batches of 24 loaves and completed
13 batches. Quality control rejected 8% of the day's total production (the
number of rejected loaves is rounded **down** to a whole loaf). Every loaf that
passed quality control was sold by closing time at the regular price of $6.50
per loaf — except that 40 of those loaves left the store under a two-for-one
promotion, meaning customers paid for only half of those 40.

The other six stores sold a combined 1,912 loaves that day, and the chain's
delivery van covered 148 km.

How much sourdough revenue (in dollars) did store #4 take in on Saturday?
Show your working. Reply with the dollar amount as a plain number.

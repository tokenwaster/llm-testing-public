A farmer must ferry a wolf, a goat, and a cabbage across a river.

- If left together **without the farmer**, the wolf eats the goat, and the
  goat eats the cabbage. (Nothing is ever eaten while the farmer is present,
  including in the boat.)
- The boat carries the farmer plus **up to two** of the three items.
- Every crossing of the river — in either direction, loaded or empty —
  counts as one crossing.

Read the constraints carefully; this is not quite the puzzle you may
remember. What is the **minimum total number of crossings** the farmer needs
to get all three items safely to the far side? Show the crossing plan, then
reply with the number.

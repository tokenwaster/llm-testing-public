A statistician walks into a room and wants to
know how likely it is that someone in the room shares a birthday with HER
specifically (not with each other — with her).

Assume 365 equally likely birthdays, ignore leap years, and that she knows
her own birthday. What is the minimum number of OTHER people that must be in
the room so that the probability at least one of them shares HER birthday
exceeds 50%?

Think carefully about exactly which event is being asked about.

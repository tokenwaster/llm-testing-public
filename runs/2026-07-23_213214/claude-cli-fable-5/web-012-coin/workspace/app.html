About the Mint

A proof-of-work you can hold.

WastedToken Mint began as a joke between engineers: if we're going to burn ten thousand tokens on a single conversation, the least the universe owes us is a coin. So we built the dies, bought the press, and made the joke permanent.

Each piece is die-struck from solid 2.4 oz brass, milled with a traditional reeded edge, and finished by hand in one of four metal treatments. The obverse carries the Mint's monogram in raised relief; the reverse states the denomination — the count of tokens it commemorates — because a number that big deserves to be embossed, not logged.

Editions are struck once and retired. When a die is retired it is defaced on camera and archived. No re-strikes, no reprints, no mercy. If you missed the Gold Reserve, so did everyone else who hesitated.

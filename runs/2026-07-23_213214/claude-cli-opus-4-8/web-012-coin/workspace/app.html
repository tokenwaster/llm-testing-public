The Mint

We waste nothing. We token everything.

WastedToken Mint began in a two-press garage workshop with a stubborn idea: that a coin you can hold in your fist beats a thousand you can only screenshot. We design in-house, cut our own dies, and strike every piece under real tonnage. What comes out is heavy, cold, and honest.

01

Deep-relief dies

Each emblem is hand-sculpted then hard-tooled, so the raised WastedToken crest catches light from across a room — not a flat etch, a real embossed hill of metal.

02

Numbered runs

Editions are capped and serialized on the reeded edge. When the run is struck, the die is retired. Sold out means sold out — no repress, no reissue, ever.

03

Redeemable tokens

Every coin is stamped with a token denomination. Stack them, trade them, gift them at the bar. They mean whatever your crew decides they mean — and they weigh exactly what a promise should.

The Craft

From sketch to strike

No two mints do it the same. Here's ours, start to shipping box.

1

Sculpt

The crest is modeled in high relief, every ridge and serif carved for maximum light-catch.

2

Tool

Dies are cut in hardened tool steel, then reeded so the edge bites the light as it turns.

3

Strike

Blanks are pressed under full tonnage — a single hit, deep and permanent.

4

Number

Each coin is serialized on the edge, capsuled, and boxed. Then the die retires.

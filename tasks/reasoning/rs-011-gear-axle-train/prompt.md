A gear train has 9 gears in a line. Two gears can be connected two ways:
- **Meshing**: their teeth engage. A meshed gear's speed is the other gear's speed times (other's teeth / this gear's teeth).
- **Same shaft**: two gears fixed on one axle turn at the SAME rpm (a shared shaft is not a mesh — no tooth ratio applies).

Gear 1 is the driver: it has 18 teeth and turns at 180 rpm.
Gear 2 (15 teeth) MESHES with gear 1.
Gear 3 (24 teeth) MESHES with gear 2.
Gear 4 (24 teeth) MESHES with gear 3.
Gear 5 (18 teeth) MESHES with gear 4.
Gear 6 (36 teeth) MESHES with gear 5.
Gear 7 (24 teeth) is fixed on the SAME SHAFT as gear 6.
Gear 8 (18 teeth) MESHES with gear 7.
Gear 9 (30 teeth) MESHES with gear 8.

What is the rpm of **gear 9**? Reply with the number (2 decimals), e.g. `73.50`.

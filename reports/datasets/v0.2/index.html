Capable is how many of the fleet clear the bar — when it reads 35/35 the lane is saturated and a score ranking is meaningless (they all tie), so the real answer is the cheapest that works: you do not need a frontier model for that job. When Capable is low, the lane genuinely separates and Best is the answer. Cheapest counts only durable costs — a local model's measured electricity, or a genuinely paid API. OpenRouter's promotional :free variants are listed beneath it as “free now” and marked ⏳: their $0 is a true record of what that run was billed, but it is a promotion, not a price you can plan on (why this matters →). Filter to the models you can actually run. Top quality = highest category score (ties share the crown). Value pick = fastest generator among models scoring at least the capable threshold — the rational choice when latency or bulk throughput matters more than the last few points. Thresholds live in directives.yaml (excellent ≥ 0.95, capable ≥ 0.85, value pick ≥ 120 tok/s) — edit and the classification updates on the next report regeneration.
